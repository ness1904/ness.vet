About

Veteran | Senior Systems Engineer | CISSP | PMP — focused on mission communications, secure architectures, and disciplined delivery in high-reliability environments.

What I Do

I advise defense teams operating in mission-critical communications environments—where RF, tactical datalinks, and ground systems intersect with security constraints and program risk. My work emphasizes resilient architectures, integration clarity, and decision-ready guidance.

I bring a systems-first approach: define boundaries, reduce ambiguity early, align stakeholders, and validate readiness under real-world constraints.

Operating Principles

  • Mission-first execution: outcomes and readiness over optics or activity.
  • Security as a constraint: identity, trust boundaries, and auditability designed in from day one.
  • Governance matters: decision authority, “definition of done,” and controlled change management.
  • Integration discipline: ownership, interfaces, and rollback plans defined before field deployment.

Background

My professional foundation was built in the United States Marine Corps supporting mission-critical aviation systems within operational squadrons. That environment demanded precision, accountability, and disciplined execution under pressure.

Since then, my work has expanded across RF field operations, datalink environments, and ground station operational support — translating operational realities into secure architectures, program clarity, and resilient delivery.

Credentials & Focus

  • Certifications: CISSP, PMP
  • Education: MS — Unmanned Systems
  • Focus areas: mission communications, secure architecture, program governance, operational resilience

Note: I do not publish sensitive program details. This site focuses on principles, operating approach, and professional capabilities.

Engagement

I’m available for consulting engagements involving mission communications strategy, secure architecture review, integration risk reduction, and program governance during critical delivery phases.
