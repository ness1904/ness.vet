About

Veteran | Senior Systems Engineer | CISSP — focused on RF & datalink systems integration, secure mission architectures, and disciplined delivery in high-reliability environments.

What I Do

I operate at the intersection of mission communications and cybersecurity: integrating RF, tactical datalink, and ground station systems within secure mission architectures. My experience includes ground station operational support in high-reliability environments where uptime, latency, and security posture directly impact mission execution.

I focus on disciplined integration — aligning hardware, networks, encryption boundaries, and operational workflows so systems function cohesively under real-world conditions. Execution is structured, risk-informed, and grounded in measurable acceptance criteria.

Operating Principles

  • Mission-first execution: outcomes and readiness over optics or activity.
  • Security as a constraint: identity, trust boundaries, and auditability designed in from day one.
  • Integration discipline: ownership, interfaces, and rollback plans defined before field deployment.
  • Clarity under pressure: decision authority, “definition of done,” and controlled change management.

Background

My professional foundation was built in the United States Marine Corps, supporting mission-critical aviation systems within operational squadrons. That environment demanded precision, accountability, and disciplined execution under pressure.

Since then, my work has expanded across RF field operations, datalink environments, and ground station operational support — coordinating engineering, cybersecurity, and field teams to maintain readiness and deliver resilient mission capability.

Credentials & Focus

  • Certifications: CISSP, PMP
  • Education: MS — Unmanned Systems
  • Focus areas: RF & datalink integration, secure architectures, mission-critical engineering

Engagement
